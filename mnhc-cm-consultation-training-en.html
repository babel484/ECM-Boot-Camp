🎙 Episode 1 · Section 1 — Why It Matters
Care Management Consultation — What It Is and Why It Matters
Brian and Anna explain when to use a Consultation encounter, how it differs from a Step-Up, and why using the wrong encounter type creates billing, audit, and compliance risk.

What Is a Care Management Consultation?

Used when receiving a case from another ICM discipline — Ryan White, Behavioral Health, Disease Management, Resource Connection, or Prevention and Wellness — or bridging a patient between programs within the service line. Establishes the care team, creates or updates the program episode, documents the care plan, and records the Level of Service. Distinct from Step-Up, which is reserved for brand-new ECM patients.

Step-Up vs. Consultation

  • Step-Up: New patient entering ECM for the first time.
  • Consultation: Patient already in system; received from another discipline or bridging programs.
  • Never use Consultation to avoid Step-Up for a genuinely new patient.

When to Use a Consultation — Scenarios & SmartPhrases
Brian walks through the two key clinical scenarios that trigger a Consultation encounter and introduces the two SmartPhrases that drive all documentation.
SmartPhrase Quick Reference
SmartPhraseWhen to UseWhat It Captures
.CAREMANAGEMENTCONSULTOutreach successful — spoke to patientContact details, care coordination activities, plan components, next steps
.CALAIMUNSUCCESSFULOutreach unsuccessful — unable to reach patientAttempt documentation, method used, next contact date

Scenario A — Cross-Discipline Referral

A Ryan White CM hands off to Disease Management for a patient whose HIV is stable but diabetes now needs focused management. Or Behavioral Health bridges to Resource Connection once mental health is stabilized and SDoH support becomes the priority. The receiving CM opens a Consultation to accept the case and document the handoff.

Scenario B — Program Bridge

A patient transitions between ICM programs — for example, from Prevention and Wellness into Disease Management as chronic condition complexity increases, or from Behavioral Health into Resource Connection for SDoH wraparound support. The Consultation documents the bridge and establishes the correct LOS for the new program context.

🎙 Episode 3 · Section 2 — Steps 1–5
Steps 1–5: Log In, Find Patient, Create Encounter & Program
Brian walks through logging in, locating the patient chart, opening the Patient Outreach encounter, completing the Initiation tab, and setting up or linking the program episode.
1
Log Into Epic
  • Open Epic and log in with your credentials.
2
Find Your Patient
  • Navigate to the Chart section and search for your patient by name or MRN.
3
Create a Patient Outreach Encounter
  • Select Patient Outreach as the encounter type.
  • Add yourself as the encounter provider and click Accept.
⚠ Always use Patient Outreach. Never substitute another encounter type — this is a compliance requirement and will be audited.
4
Initiation Tab — Contacts & Care Teams
  • Input Contacts: Contact Name, Relationship, Phone Number, Outcome (all required).
  • Scroll to Care Teams → add yourself. Set Role: Lead Care Manager. Set notifications to Yes → click Accept.
5
Care Mgmt Tab — Link or Create Program
  • Billable ECM Consultation: Check Linked checkbox on existing CalAIM ECM program. Confirm Status = Enrolled.
  • Non-Billable / Cross-Discipline: Click +New Program → Program: Case Management · Enrollment Reason: Care Coordination · Status: Outreach.
  • Always disenroll incorrect programs before creating a new one.
⚠ For ECM Consultation: link existing program, do not create a new one. For non-billable cross-discipline work: create new Case Management program.
🎙 Episode 4 · Section 2 — Steps 6–8
Steps 6–8: Track Outreach, Care Plan & Diagnosis
Brian covers documenting the outreach in Track Pt Outreach, selecting the correct Care Plan based on billability, and entering the required Visit Diagnosis (Dx Code 1312356).
6
Track Patient Outreach
  • Navigate to Track Pt Outreach area in Care Management section.
  • Contacted Via: Telephone · Contacted About: Care Coordination · Set Next Contact date.
7
Add Care Plan
  • Billable: Use Cal Aim Care Plan.
  • Non-billable: Use Blank Care Plan and update the name appropriately.
  • Include the problems being consulted on and add relevant tasks.
8
Add Visit Diagnoses
  • Primary Dx: Dx Code 1312356 — Encounter for counseling for care management of chronic conditions and complex health needs using nurse-based model.
  • Add all other relevant diagnoses from the Problem List.
🔔 If a patient screens positive for Food Insecurity, add Food Insecurity as an additional Dx alongside the primary code.
🎙 Episode 5 · Section 3 — Steps 9–11
Steps 9–11: Level of Service, SmartPhrase & Sign
The most error-prone section — Brian explains CHG15 vs CHG12/13, when to enter the SmartPhrase, and how provider authorization and signing complete the encounter.
9
Select Level of Service (LOS)
  • CHG15 — Initial outreach encounter (first outreach in this episode)
  • CHG12 / CHG13 — Enrolled case started by another provider (telephonic/electronic)
  • CHG08 / CHG10 — Enrolled case, in-person clinical or physician-coordinated care
⚠ CHG15 vs CHG12/13 is not interchangeable. When in doubt, ask your supervisor before signing.
10
Enter SmartPhrase
  • Successful outreach: .CAREMANAGEMENTCONSULT
  • Unsuccessful outreach: .CALAIMUNSUCCESSFUL
⚠ Complete ALL prior steps before entering the SmartPhrase. It pulls from those fields — empty fields = incomplete note.
11
Provider Authorization & Sign
  • Provider authorization: PCP
  • Review the note, then sign the encounter.
  • Never leave encounters unsigned.

🎙 Episode 7 · Section 4 — Compliance
Documentation Standards & Compliance
Brian and Anna cover the three most common documentation errors, HIPAA compliance requirements, audit expectations, and what to do when something doesn't look right.

Common Errors to Avoid

  • Wrong encounter type (anything other than Patient Outreach)
  • Incorrect LOS code selection
  • SmartPhrase entered before completing upstream steps
  • Unsigned encounters left in the system
  • Using Consultation for a genuinely new patient (must use Step-Up)
  • Missing Care Team assignment
  • Missing Next Contact date

Documentation Standards

  • Always use Patient Outreach as the encounter type
  • Do not use any alternative SmartPhrases
  • Documentation must be timely, accurate, and HIPAA-compliant
  • Do not leave encounters unsigned
  • When in doubt — ask your supervisor before signing
  • Contact PHQI Analyst for technical issues with Epic tools
